Meaning

In statistics, the coefficient of determination, denoted R2 and pronounced “R squared”, is the proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variable(s).

Suppose R2 = 0.49. This implies that 49% of the variability of the dependent variable has been accounted for, and the remaining 51% of the variability is still unaccounted for.
